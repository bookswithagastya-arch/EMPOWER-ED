Built from observation.

EmpowerED started from two years of observation — not a classroom theory. Here's where it came from and where it's going.

A
Agastya
Founder, EmpowerED
DPS Bangalore South

The problem isn't capability. It's always been the system.

I spent two years in Thirthahalli, Shivamogga during COVID — long enough to see what actually happens in rural classrooms. Students weren't struggling because they lacked ability. They were disengaging because nothing in the curriculum felt like it belonged to their world.

The textbooks used words from cities. The pictures showed things they'd never seen. The teaching method was rote repetition — write this word twenty times and somehow you'll remember it. It wasn't working, and everyone knew it wasn't working.

"The problem isn't capability. It's always been the system — and the incentives that shape it."

Before EmpowerED started, i tutored six students in Thirthahalli. I wanted to understand what actually worked — not what the curriculum said should work. What I found was simple: when the material felt like their world, students engaged. When it felt foreign, they switched off.

After a second cohort of twenty students, it was clear that individual tutoring — however well-intentioned — doesn't scale. The goal became building something any teacher could use in any rural classroom, without needing me to be there.

How we got here

2020–2022
Two years in Thirthahalli
Lived in Thirthahalli during COVID. Observed first-hand how rural students engaged with English education — and why they often didn't.
2023
Trial 1 — 6 students
First structured teaching sessions in Thirthahalli. Tested what approaches worked and what didn't.
2024
Trial 2 — 20 students
Scaled the approach, refined the curriculum, and began documenting outcomes.
2025
EmpowerED curriculum launched
First structured curriculum with games, worksheets, Kannada explanations, and lesson plans — built to be used by any teacher.
Summer 2026
Out soon

The long-term goal

A free, replicable English curriculum that any rural Karnataka teacher can download and use — without training, without cost, and without needing anyone from outside to come in and run it. EmpowerED belongs to the communities it serves.
