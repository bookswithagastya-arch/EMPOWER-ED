Built in the field

The gap isn't in capability. It's in context.

Rural Karnataka students technically learn English in school — but standard curricula use foreign words, foreign pictures, and foreign examples. Students don't disengage because they can't learn. They disengage because nothing feels relevant to them.

📚

Textbooks feel foreign

Generic words and Western images create no connection to a child's actual world.

🔄

Rote repetition kills curiosity

Writing the same word 20 times teaches endurance, not language.

🌉

No bridge from Kannada

Students are expected to jump into English with no support from their mother tongue.

Our approach
Three principles that make it work
EmpowerED isn't a different textbook. It's a different philosophy — built from two years of observation in Thirthahalli.
01

Their world, their words

Every word family uses vocabulary from rural Karnataka life — mangoes, rain, markets, cricket. Students recognise the world in the material before they can read it.

02

Games over drills

Every lesson has a game at its core — market games, word cricket, story relay. Competitive energy and play create the engagement that repetition never could.

03

Kannada as a bridge

Explanations in Kannada so students feel safe. All targets in English so they keep moving forward. Respect for their language is the foundation of trust.

How we learn
Every lesson has a game at its core
Because curiosity, not repetition, is what builds lasting language.
🏏

Word family cricket

Bowler calls a word family. Batter hits by naming a word from it. Three misses and you're out. Every Karnataka kid already knows the rules.

🛒

Market game

Pretend market stalls with word labels. Students "buy" items by reading them correctly. Uses the market context they know from daily life.

📖

Story relay

One student writes one sentence. The next continues the story. Forces reading what came before — comprehension built through play.

🎯

True or false race

Teacher reads a sentence. Students run to opposite sides of the room for true or false. Physical movement breaks the sitting monotony.

🎡

Spin the word

A spinning wheel with pictures. Land on one, say the word, spell it, use it in a sentence. Competitive energy keeps everyone alert.

✏️

Draw and write

Students draw something from their village then label it using lesson vocabulary.

About

Built in Thirthahalli. Designed for the students I grew up with.

I spent two years in Thirthahalli, Shivamogga during COVID — long enough to see what actually happens in rural classrooms. Students weren't struggling because they lacked ability. They were disengaging because nothing in the curriculum felt like it belonged to their world.

"The problem isn't capability. It's always been the system — and the incentives that shape it."

EmpowerED started as three sessions with six students. It became a structured curriculum after I realised that individual tutoring, however well-intentioned, doesn't scale. The goal was always to build something any teacher could use — in any rural classroom, without needing me to be there.

This is still being built. Cohort 3 launches this summer in Thirthahalli. School partnerships are in progress. Everything here is real, tested, and improving.
