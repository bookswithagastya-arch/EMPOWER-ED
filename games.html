Every lesson has a game at its core

Because curiosity, not repetition, is what builds lasting language. Each game is designed around contexts rural Karnataka students already know and love.

🏏
Reading · All levels

Word family cricket

Uses the sport every Karnataka kid already knows to make word families competitive and fun.

How to play: Teacher bowls by calling a word family. Students bat by naming a valid word from that family. Three misses and you're out. Class keeps score.
🛒
Reading · Grade 1–3

Market game

Uses the market context students know from daily life — buying, selling, haggling — to make word recognition feel real.

How to play: Set up pretend market stalls, each with a word label. Students "buy" items by reading the label correctly. Wrong reading means no purchase.
📖
Comprehension · Grade 2–5

Story relay

Forces students to read what came before them and build on it — comprehension and creativity in one activity.

How to play: Student 1 writes one sentence. Student 2 reads it and adds the next. Continue around the class. The story must make sense.
🎯
Comprehension · All levels

True or false race

Physical movement breaks sitting monotony and makes comprehension checks into a whole-body activity.

How to play: Teacher reads a sentence. Students run to one side for true, the other for false. Fast and loud — keeps energy high.
🎡
Vocabulary · Grade 1–4

Spin the word

Competitive rotation keeps every student alert — nobody knows who the wheel will land on next.

How to play: Spin a wheel with pictures. Whatever it lands on, that student must say the word, spell it, and use it in a sentence.
✏️
Writing · Grade 2–5

Draw and write

Students draw something from their own village — a tree, a market stall, a cow — then label it in English.

How to play: Give students 10 minutes to draw freely. Then they label every element they can name in English using lesson vocabulary.
